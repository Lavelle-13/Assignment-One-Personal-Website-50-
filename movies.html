Here are some of my favourite movies and a short paragraph about each.

Interstellar
Interstellar is just breathtaking, it's the perfect blend of heart and mind. The way it dives into space exploration while staying rooted in the emotional core of a father-daughter bond is genius. Hans Zimmer's score sends shivers down your spine, the visuals of wormholes and distant planets are mesmerizing, and the science—while complex—is treated with such respect that it feels almost tangible. Every time I watch it, I get this mix of awe, hope, and a little existential panic about how vast the universe is. Truly a masterpiece that lingers long after the credits roll.
Pulp Fiction
Pulp Fiction is absolute cinematic chaos in the best way possible. Tarantino's dialogue is razor-sharp and endlessly quotable, every scene crackles with tension, humor, and unpredictability. The non-linear storytelling keeps you on your toes, weaving hitmen, gangsters, and unforgettable characters into this wild tapestry. And that soundtrack? Iconic. It's stylish, darkly funny, and endlessly cool—a movie that feels like it was made to be rewatched over and over, each time catching something new.
The Lorax
The Lorax is such a vibrant, heartfelt gem. It's playful and colorful on the surface, but it hits hard with its environmental message—Truffula trees, Once-ler's mistakes, and the Lorax himself all stick with you. The way it balances whimsy, humor, and a real sense of urgency about caring for the planet is brilliant. Every time I watch it, I can't help but smile, laugh, and also feel inspired to do better for nature.
